Understanding temporal clustering

What is Burstiness?

The burstiness index quantifies how clustered or regular events are in a time sequence. It measures deviation from randomness in timing of events.

Formula: B = (σ - μ) / (σ + μ)

Where σ is standard deviation of inter-event intervals and μ is mean.

Interpretation:

  • B = -1: Perfect regularity (events at uniform intervals)
  • B = 0: Random (Poisson process)
  • B = +1: Extreme burstiness (highly clustered)

Understanding Burstiness

Regular (B = -1)
Events occur at perfectly uniform intervals. Think of a metronome or scheduled train arrivals.
Random (B = 0)
Events follow a Poisson process. Inter-event intervals are exponentially distributed.
Bursty (B = +1)
Events cluster in bursts with long gaps. Common in social communications and neural spikes.
